The BigChange Demonstration Guide

A practical framework for a successful Field Service Management solution demo.

Deep Dive Research

  • Investigate: Use the internet (Companies House, Website) to find services, roles, and employee numbers.
  • Preparation: Refresh knowledge the day before. Verify all hardware (mobile device, tablet, headphones) and software are charged and working.
  • Pre-Check: Do not ask questions you could have researched (e.g., "What services do you offer?").

Contextualize Your Data

  • Crucial Match: Use data *specific* to the customer's industry (e.g., Grounds Maintenance data for a GM company).
  • NEVER: Say, "I know this isn't your data, but you get the idea." They won't.
  • Personalize CRM: Find their address on Google Maps, then create a customer *very close* to their location in the CRM to show preparation.

Focus & Time Control

  • The 30-Minute Rule: Out of 45 minutes, realistically only 30 minutes are for the demo itself (after intros/conclusions).
  • Show Less, Mean More: Focus on what's critical. It's not what you show, but what you don't show that makes the presentation worthwhile.
  • Be the Leader: You must lead the meeting. It is easy to be "hijacked," making it difficult to turn the discussion back around.

Build Trust

  • People Buy From People: Trust is key. If the buyer doesn't "gel" with the seller, the deal may fail.
  • Dress Code: Dress smart, and wear BigChange branded wear if possible.
  • Clarify: Do not assume. Always ask for clarification of unknown or unusual terms.
  • Agenda: If possible, have and stick to a pre-defined agenda.

Be Honest, Follow Up

  • Valid Answer: "I do not know" is a valid answer.
  • The Caveat: You MUST agree to find the answer and follow up (e.g., "I will get one of my technical people to investigate and get back to you.").
  • Crucial Action: YOU MUST GET BACK TO THEM.

Optimize & Strategize

  • Avoid: Meetings with too many people from multiple departments (can become a shambles).
  • Be Wary Of: Meetings led by the IT team (they may not have the operational team's best interests at heart).
  • Remember: The MEDDICC sales qualification framework.

Key Takeaway

A successful BigChange demo is built on trust, preparation, and relevance. Customize the experience, manage the time, and always, always follow through on your commitments.
