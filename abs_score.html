Understanding the ABS Score

The **Annual Bill Savings (ABS) score** is the engine of the UK's ECO4 scheme, designed to fund deep energy retrofits for homes. This interactive guide breaks down what it is, how it's calculated, and why it matters for homeowners, installers, and the UK's net-zero goals.

The Retrofit Journey

Achieving an ABS score involves a standardized, multi-step process governed by the PAS 2035 framework, ensuring quality and compliance from start to finish.

1
Retrofit Assessment

A qualified assessor conducts a whole-house survey, gathering data on insulation, heating, and condition to establish the starting SAP score.

2
Project Coordination

A Retrofit Coordinator designs a comprehensive improvement plan, ensuring all measures work together effectively and meet PAS 2035 standards.

3
Installation & Verification

Accredited installers carry out the work. Each measure is lodged in the TrustMark Data Warehouse, allowing for tracking and quality assurance.

4
Final Scoring

After a post-retrofit assessment confirms the new SAP score, the final Full Project Score (FPS) is calculated and awarded.

The Big Picture: Strategy and Stakeholders

The ABS score is more than a calculation; it's a policy tool designed to reshape the retrofit market and impact everyone involved.

From ECO3 to ECO4: A Major Shift

The move from Lifetime Bill Savings (LBS) to Annual Bill Savings (ABS) was a deliberate strategy to incentivize deeper, more impactful retrofits.

ECO3

Lifetime Savings (LBS)

Valued single measures over their lifetime, often leading to shallow, piecemeal upgrades.

ECO4

Annual Savings (ABS)

Values the **annual** saving from a **whole-house** upgrade, pushing for significant EPC band improvements.

Challenges & Real-World Gaps

While the ABS score is a robust theoretical model, its real-world implementation faces significant hurdles that can impact outcomes.

Key Systemic Issues

Constrained Supply Chain

There is a shortage of qualified and accredited installers, making it difficult to deliver retrofits at the scale and speed required to meet ECO4 targets.

High Upfront Costs

The costs for detailed assessments, coordination, and compliance with PAS 2035 are high, discouraging smaller installers and single-measure jobs.

Quality Control Concerns

Reports from homeowners highlight issues with workmanship and project management, indicating a need for stronger oversight and accountability.

The "Performance Gap"

The ABS score is a "deemed" saving, based on models. Actual savings can be lower due to installation quality, occupant behavior, and other real-world factors.

Glossary of Key Terms

A quick reference for the core concepts in the ECO4 and retrofit landscape.

ABS (Annual Bill Savings)

The core metric of ECO4, representing the estimated annual financial saving from energy efficiency measures.

SAP (Standard Assessment Procedure)

The UK's official methodology for calculating a home's energy performance, resulting in a score from 1-100+.

EPC (Energy Performance Certificate)

A user-friendly certificate that displays the SAP score in bands from A (most efficient) to G (least efficient).

FPS (Full Project Score)

The final, full score awarded when a retrofit project meets the minimum required EPC band improvement.

PPS (Partial Project Score)

A deflated, interim score paid for individual measures to help installer cash flow during a long project.

PAS 2035

The overarching standard and framework for carrying out high-quality, safe, and effective domestic retrofits in the UK.
