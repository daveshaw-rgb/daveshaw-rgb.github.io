Introduction

This document serves as a handover guide for the Retrofit Scotland project, an energy solutions provider aiming to transition from manual, disparate processes to a unified, end-to-end workflow management system. The core objective is to solve the current problem of lost lead visibility and inefficient tracking by implementing a live, automated system. The proposed BigChange platform will provide a single source of truth for all project data, improving efficiency, compliance, and accountability from lead generation to job completion.

Pain Points

  • Manual Processes: The current system relies heavily on manual work, using a combination of Dropbox, Excel spreadsheets, and a third-party app called Service Mate.
  • Loss of Visibility: It is easy to lose track of leads, with a potential for 50-60 leads per month being lost due to a lack of live tracking.
  • Lack of Real-Time Data: The Excel trackers only provide notes and don't offer a real-time, active reminder system for tasks.
  • No Centralized System: All different parts of the business—admin, submissions, surveyors, and installers—use separate platforms, which is inefficient and chaotic.

Current Systems & Processes

  • Current Systems in Use: Dropbox for storing documents and photos, Excel spreadsheets for tracking, and Service Mate for monitoring installers.
  • Integration Requirements with BigChange: No, this is a transition to a new end-to-end system to replace the current manual processes.

Proposed Solution

The proposed solution utilizes the BigChange platform to provide a comprehensive, integrated, and end-to-end workflow management system. The architecture is based on a series of interlinked jobs, notes, and custom automations. It addresses the client's pain points by providing a single source for managing leads, scheduling, and compliance. The workflow begins with a comprehensive 'ECO LEAD' note which, upon completion, automatically creates a survey job. This job includes configurable worksheets for surveyors to complete on-site, with mandatory steps for compliance. The system will also handle the generation of final reports and manage job submission to funders, providing real-time visibility throughout the entire lifecycle.

Functional Requirements Summary

  • Scheduling Scenarios:
    • Job Types: Confirmed job types include 'survey' and 'installation.'
    • Scheduling Patterns: The system needs to support scheduling individual jobs (e.g., surveys) and group jobs (e.g., installation, with different resources for different parts of the job).
    • Edge Cases/Complex Scenarios: The solution needs to handle scenarios where not all evidence is collected on the initial survey, and it should provide manual modification capabilities for worksheet answers.
  • Financial Requirements:
    • Pricing Logic: A per-user per-month fee.
    • Reporting Requirements: The ability to track a total monthly 'ABS score' which is a cumulative value from individual jobs. This data will be handled by exporting a custom report and totaling in a spreadsheet. Standard reporting and dashboards are also required for monitoring job performance and status.
  • Automations Requirements: The proof of concept included several custom automations that will be replicated in the live environment. These include:
    • ECO4 - Create Job from ECO4 Note
    • ECO4 - Set Tag to Awaiting Survey
    • ECO4 - Set tag to Qualifies
    • ECO4 - Set tag to Does Not Qualify
    • ECO4 - Set tag to Survey Booked
    • ECO4 - Set Tag to Confirmation Sent
    • ECO4 - Set Tag to Survey Visit Confirmed
    • ECO4 - Set measures requested on survey worksheet
